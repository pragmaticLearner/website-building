The Mongolian invasion of Japan took place in 1274 and 1281, when the Mongol Empire attempted to invade Japan. The invasions were led by Kublai Khan, the grandson of Genghis Khan, who sought to conquer Japan as part of his efforts to expand the Mongol Empire. The first invasion was repelled by a severe typhoon, which the Japanese referred to as "divine wind" or "kamikaze." The second invasion was also unsuccessful due to logistical problems and strong Japanese resistance. In the end, the Mongolian invasions failed, and Japan was able to maintain its independence.
